Chilaquiles Casserole

45m 10 servings 240 Calories
1 tablespoon canola oil
1 medium onion, diced
1 medium zucchini, thinly sliced
1 19-ounce can black beans, rinsed
1 14-ounce can diced tomatoes, drained
1/2 cups corn, frozen (thawed) or fresh
1 teaspoon ground cummin
1/2 teaspoon salt
12 corn tortillas, quartered
1 19-ounce caned mild red or green enchilada sauce
1/4 cups shredded reduced-fat Cheddar cheese

Directions

Prep 15m

1. Preheat ovent to 400F. Lightly coat a 9-by-13-inch baking pan with cooking spray.

2. Heat oil in a larg nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion and cook, stirring often, until starting to brown, about 5 minutes. Stir in zucchini, beans, tomatoes, corn, cumin, and salt and cook, stirring occasionally, ultil the vegetables are heated through, about 3 minutes.

3. Scatter half the tortilla pieces in the pan. Top with half the vegetable mixture, half the enchilada sauce and half the chesse. Repeat with one more layer of tortillas, vegetables, sauce and chesse. Cover with foil.

4 Bake the casserole for 15 minutes. Remove the foil and continue baking until the casserole is bublling around the edges and the cheese is melted, about 10 minutes more.
